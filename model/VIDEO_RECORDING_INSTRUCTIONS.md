## 🎯 Overview

This assessment uses AI models trained on skeletal movement patterns to predict ADOS (Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule) scores. The models analyze body movements during specific actions to assess:
- **Severity of Autism**
- **Social Affect**
- **Restricted and Repetitive Behaviors (RRB)**
- **ADOS Comparison Score**

### Why Multiple Videos?
Recording multiple actions over several days provides:
- **Behavioral consistency**: Captures typical patterns, not one-time occurrences
- **Comprehensive assessment**: Different actions reveal different behavioral indicators
- **Reliability**: Multiple samples improve prediction accuracy
- **Natural variation**: Accounts for daily fluctuations in behavior

## 🛠️ Required Equipment

### Essential:
- ✅ **Camera**: Smartphone, tablet, or webcam
  - Minimum: 720p resolution (1280×720)
  - Recommended: 1080p or higher
  - Frame rate: 30 fps (frames per second)
  
- ✅ **Space**: Clear area, minimum 2m × 2m (6.5ft × 6.5ft)
  - Unobstructed floor space
  - No furniture in the way
  
- ✅ **Lighting**: Good, even lighting
  - Natural daylight preferred
  - Avoid direct sunlight creating harsh shadows
  - Indoor lighting: use multiple light sources

### Recommended:
- 📱 Tripod or phone stand (for stable camera)
- 🎬 Plain backdrop or clear wall (reduces visual clutter)
- 👕 Fitted, solid-color clothing (improves pose detection)

## 📹 Recording Setup

### Camera Placement
```
                    [Camera]
                       |
                       | 2-4 meters
                       |
                    [Subject]
                  (Full body visible)
```

**Position Guidelines:**
1. **Distance**: 2-4 meters (6-13 feet) from subject
2. **Height**: Camera at chest/shoulder level
3. **Angle**: Directly facing the subject (front view)
4. **Framing**: Entire body visible from head to feet
5. **Stability**: Use tripod or stable surface (avoid handheld shaking)

### Environment Setup
- **Background**: Plain wall or uncluttered space
- **Floor**: Non-slip surface for safety
- **Lighting**: Even lighting from front or sides (not from behind)
- **Distractions**: Remove pets, other people, and noisy objects
- **Temperature**: Comfortable room temperature

### Subject Preparation
- **Clothing**: Fitted clothing (not baggy), short sleeves preferred
- **Footwear**: Barefoot or non-slip socks/shoes
- **Hair**: Tied back if long (to avoid obscuring face/shoulders)
- **Accessories**: Remove large jewelry, bulky items

## 🏃 Actions to Perform

### Overview of Actions
Record **6 core actions** that reveal key behavioral patterns. Each action should be performed naturally without overthinking.

---

### **Action 1: Arm Swinging**
**Purpose**: Assesses motor coordination, rhythm, and bilateral movement

**How to Perform**:
1. Stand facing the camera
2. Swing both arms forward and backward naturally
3. Like a walking motion, but standing in place
4. Maintain a steady rhythm
5. Keep arms relatively straight

**Duration**: 8-10 seconds  
**Repetitions**: 12-15 swings  
**Key Points**: Natural rhythm, symmetric movements

---

### **Action 2: Chest Expansion (Breathing)**
**Purpose**: Evaluates postural control and body awareness

**How to Perform**:
1. Stand upright facing the camera
2. Take deep breaths, expanding chest visibly
3. Arms can rest at sides or slightly away from body
4. Inhale slowly, exhale slowly
5. Keep shoulders relaxed

**Duration**: 10-12 seconds  
**Repetitions**: 5-8 breathing cycles  
**Key Points**: Visible chest movement, steady breathing

---

### **Action 3: Hand Movements**
**Purpose**: Detects self-stimulatory behaviors and fine motor patterns

**How to Perform**:
1. Stand or sit facing the camera
2. Perform natural hand movements:
   - Hand flapping (opening and closing hands)
   - Wrist rotations
   - Finger movements
   - Hand gestures
3. Move hands naturally as feels comfortable

**Duration**: 8-10 seconds  
**Repetitions**: Continuous natural movements  
**Key Points**: Natural, unforced movements; any repetitive patterns

---

### **Action 4: Arm Raising**
**Purpose**: Assesses range of motion and motor control

**How to Perform**:
1. Stand facing the camera
2. Raise both arms up above head
3. Lower arms back down to sides
4. Can be simultaneous or alternating
5. Maintain steady pace

**Duration**: 8-10 seconds  
**Repetitions**: 10-12 raises  
**Key Points**: Full range of motion, controlled movements

---

### **Action 5: Walking**
**Purpose**: Evaluates gait patterns and overall motor coordination

**How to Perform**:
1. Walk naturally forward within camera frame
2. Turn around and walk back
3. Arms swing naturally
4. Maintain natural walking pace
5. Stay within camera view

**Duration**: 10-12 seconds  
**Repetitions**: 2-3 passes (forward and back)  
**Key Points**: Natural gait, arms swinging, stay in frame

---

### **Action 6: Standing Still**
**Purpose**: Baseline for postural stability and natural posture

**How to Perform**:
1. Stand naturally facing the camera
2. Arms relaxed at sides
3. Look forward (towards camera)
4. Breathe normally
5. Maintain natural, comfortable posture

**Duration**: 8-10 seconds  
**Repetitions**: Just stand naturally  
**Key Points**: Relaxed posture, minimal movement

## 📅 Recording Schedule

### Recommended Protocol: **3 Days, 2 Sessions per Day**

#### **Why This Schedule?**
- **Behavioral consistency**: Captures typical patterns across different times
- **Variability**: Accounts for daily fluctuations (energy levels, mood)
- **Statistical reliability**: Multiple samples improve model accuracy
- **Non-intrusive**: Short sessions prevent fatigue and frustration

---

### **Day 1 - Baseline Assessment**

#### Session 1 (Morning - 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM)
Record ALL 6 actions:
- ✅ Arm Swinging (8-10 sec)
- ✅ Chest Expansion (10-12 sec)
- ✅ Hand Movements (8-10 sec)
- ✅ Arm Raising (8-10 sec)
- ✅ Walking (10-12 sec)
- ✅ Standing Still (8-10 sec)

**Total time**: ~15-20 minutes (including setup and breaks)

#### Session 2 (Afternoon - 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM)
Repeat ALL 6 actions with same duration

**Purpose**: Capture morning vs afternoon differences

---

### **Day 2 - Follow-up Assessment** (24 hours later)

#### Session 1 (Morning - 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM)
Record **Priority Actions** (4 actions):
- ✅ Arm Swinging
- ✅ Hand Movements  
- ✅ Walking
- ✅ Standing Still

**Total time**: ~10-12 minutes

#### Session 2 (Afternoon - 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM)
Record **Priority Actions** (4 actions) - same as morning

---

### **Day 3 - Validation Assessment** (48 hours after Day 1)

#### Session 1 (Morning - 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM)
Record ALL 6 actions (same as Day 1)

#### Session 2 (Optional - if available)
Record Priority Actions or repeat any action that had technical issues

---

If the full 3-day protocol isn't feasible, minimum acceptable recording:

**Minimum total**: 16-18 videos over 1 day

## 🎬 Technical Specifications

### Video Format
- **Resolution**: Minimum 720p (1280×720), Recommended 1080p (1920×1080)
- **Frame Rate**: 30 fps (frames per second)
- **Format**: MP4, MOV, or AVI
- **Orientation**: Landscape (horizontal) preferred, Portrait acceptable if full body visible
- **Length per video**: 8-12 seconds per action

### Camera Settings (if adjustable)
- **Focus**: Auto-focus enabled or manual focus on subject
- **Exposure**: Auto-exposure (avoid overexposed or dark videos)
- **Stabilization**: Enable if available
- **Zoom**: No zoom - use natural camera view

### Audio
- **Not required**: Audio is not analyzed by the models
- **Optional**: Can be recorded for clinical notes

## 🔧 Troubleshooting

### Common Issues and Solutions

#### Problem: Subject Goes Out of Frame
**Solution**: 
- Move camera farther back (3-4 meters)
- Use wider angle camera setting
- Mark floor boundaries with tape

#### Problem: Poor Lighting / Dark Video
**Solution**:
- Record near window (natural light)
- Add lamps in front and sides
- Avoid recording with light source behind subject

#### Problem: Shaky Video
**Solution**:
- Use tripod or phone stand
- Place camera on stable surface
- Enable video stabilization if available

#### Problem: Action Not Clear
**Solution**:
- Demonstrate action first
- Provide verbal cues during recording
- Allow multiple takes if needed

#### Problem: Subject Uncomfortable or Uncooperative
**Solution**:
- Take breaks between actions
- Make it fun (e.g., "Let's play Simon Says")
- Record over multiple short sessions
- Skip actions if causing distress

#### Problem: Video File Too Large
**Solution**:
- Use MP4 format (smaller file size)
- Record at 720p instead of 4K
- Keep videos to 10-12 seconds
- Compress video after recording (maintain quality)

## ⚠️ Important Notes

### Safety First
- Ensure safe recording environment
- No slippery floors
- Remove obstacles
- Supervise young children

### Natural Behavior
- **Don't coach**: Let subject move naturally
- **Don't correct**: Natural patterns are most informative
- **Don't stress**: Comfort is more important than perfect videos

### Clinical Use
- This is a **screening tool**, not diagnostic
- Results should be interpreted by qualified clinicians
- Should supplement, not replace, comprehensive clinical assessment
